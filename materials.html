Essential Ingredients

Sacred Woods

Sandalwood - Mysore Variety Image showing raw sandalwood logs and powder

Sandalwood (Santalum album)

Origin & Sourcing

Primarily sourced from:

  • Mysore, Karnataka (India) - Premium grade
  • Western Australia - Sustainable plantations
  • Indonesia - Select varieties
Properties
  • Sweet, woody, and persistent aroma
  • Natural fixative properties
  • Slow and even burning characteristics
  • Rich in santalol compounds
  • Excellent binding capability
Traditional Usage

Core ingredient in premium incense, particularly valued for:

  • Base note in complex blends
  • Standalone premium incense
  • Sacred and meditation practices
Sustainability Notes

Protected species requiring:

  • Regulated harvesting permits
  • Minimum 20-30 year growth cycle
  • Sustainable plantation management

Makko Powder Traditional Japanese binding powder

Makko Powder (Machilus thunbergii)

Origin & Sourcing

Traditional Japanese material sourced from:

  • Machilus tree bark
  • Primarily from Japan and Taiwan
  • Sustainable harvesting practices
Properties
  • Natural adhesive properties
  • Neutral scent profile
  • Clean burning characteristics
  • Fine, consistent texture
Traditional Usage
  • Primary binding agent in Japanese incense
  • Helps control burning rate
  • Enhances fragrance dispersion

Myrrh Resin (Commiphora myrrha)

Origin & Sourcing

Ancient aromatic resin harvested from:

  • Somalia and Eastern Africa
  • Arabian Peninsula
  • Traditional harvesting regions
Properties
  • Warm, earthy aroma
  • Rich, complex scent profile
  • Excellent fixative properties
  • Natural preservative qualities
Traditional Usage

Valued in traditional and modern applications:

  • Sacred ceremonies and rituals
  • Base note in complex blends
  • Meditative practices
Sustainability Notes
  • Wild-harvested resource
  • Traditional harvesting methods
  • Community-supported collection

Understanding Materials

Sourcing & Quality

Our materials are carefully selected from traditional sources, ensuring both quality and sustainability. Each ingredient undergoes rigorous testing for:

  • Aromatic potency
  • Purity standards
  • Sustainable harvesting
  • Traditional authenticity

Storage & Handling

Proper storage is crucial for maintaining the quality of incense materials:

  • Temperature-controlled environment
  • Protection from direct sunlight
  • Appropriate humidity levels
  • Airtight containers for preservation

Traditional Uses

Understanding the historical context of these materials enriches their appreciation:

  • Ceremonial practices
  • Meditation enhancement
  • Cultural significance
  • Therapeutic applications
