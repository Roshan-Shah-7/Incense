About Traditional Incense Forms

Stick Incense

The most common form, developed in Japan and China. Known for precise burning times and consistent fragrance release.

Cone Incense

Concentrated form offering longer burning times and stronger fragrance, popular in Tibet and Nepal.

Pure Resins

The oldest form of incense, used in Middle Eastern and African traditions. Burned on charcoal or heated surfaces.
